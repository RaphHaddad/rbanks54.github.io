--- layout: post title: CQRS and What It Means for Your Architecture date: '2016-06-28T22:53:00.002+10:00' author: Richard Banks tags: modified_time: '2016-06-28T22:53:13.039+10:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-13321238.post-189709735233728721 blogger_orig_url: http://www.richard-banks.org/2016/06/cqrs-and-what-it-means-for-your.html --- I just gave a presentation on CQRS (Command Query Responsibility Separation) at the Sydney Alt.Net group and have uploaded the slides to SlideShare for you to have a look at.

Overall, it's a pretty simple pattern, but also one that can be quite powerful. It can also be the launching point for getting into Domain Driven Design and a gateway to more advanced architectural approaches such as event sourcing and microservices.

That said, CQRS does not need to be used with those and is a valuable enough on its own to be included in every developers toolkit.
